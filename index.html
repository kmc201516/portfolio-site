About Me

Hi, my name is Kay and this is my website. I like gaming, music, and chatting with friends. I am transgender and setting records in the Call of Duty scene as the best transgender player.
I have been playing video games since I was 5 and got really into Call of Duty when I was 10. I currently play competitive Call of Duty on a team called RoseBlade and I have been in the top 1% of players for 3 years in a row.
My favourite genre of music is rap. My favourite artist is Eminem and my favourite rap song is Middle Child By J. Cole.


Career in Esports

I started competing in esports tournaments in 2015 but did not compete daily until 2019 with the release of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare (2019).
I adpoted the logo seen below as my personal logo in the summer of 2020 and I switched my competitive username from "KMC" to "SiGGht". You can watch my gameplay here.

^Content Warning!^


I started Modern Warfare playing 1v1s against all the college recruits at Ottawa University to get my name out there. After playing a very close series against one of their best players, I caught the eye of their coach. He watched on and off for a few months while me and Harsh ""Kratos" Trivedi played semi pro 2v2s. After closely monitoring my practices and games for a few months, the coach asked me to train alongside their 5v5 team. I trained with them multiple times a week and they started dominating in their league matches. Other coaches started noticing that I was doing well in practice against this star team and offered me tryouts and scholarships. I was drafted in the Mock Draft as the 3rd overall pick by St. Clair College. Since I was having success in competitive 5v5, I quit 2v2s to focus on 5v5 practice. I trained with Ottawa daily until the end of the Modern Warfare 2019 competitive season. During the offseason, we all moved to Black Ops 4 to play wagers. I was not as strong in Black Ops 4 but I got a few friends together to play a wager match against the 5 best players from Ottawa. At this point, Ottawa is undefeated in the college league so all eyes were on my team to win this wager and stop the invincible team. We beat them 3-0 in a best of 5 and they asked to rematch the next day for a lot of money. We won 3-0 again and they never paid us the money they said they would. After this, I decided to stop playing Black Ops 4 and move to playing competitive Warzone. Since I had a good competitive background already and had a decent presence in the esports scene, I was teaming up with great players and once again, eyes were on me to keep up against top Warzone players. I was invited to a CDL Invitational only a few months into playing. CDL Invitationals have some of the best players in the world all in the same lobby competing to be last alive. My team focusing on positioning and we ended getting a top 3 finish and donated our $1,000 winnings to charity.

Fast forward to November 2020: Call of Duty: Cold War releases and I am expected to compete in CCL tryouts in January but I did not have plans to compete in Cold War just yet. Resurgence just released on Warzone and Harsh "Kratos" Trivedi and I teamed up again. We were dominating the ranked ladder and were climbing very quickly. We are going into the holiday break in the top 10,000 players worldwide. We kept playing and with only a few minutes left in 2020, we won a game and qualified for top 2,000. By the end of New Years Day, we were in the top 1,000 of players and I was overcome with emotions knowing my hard work paid off. I injured my wrist that week so I had to take the week off and take care of my wrist. After my wrist was better, I had to temporarily retire from competitive Warzone to move to Cold War. I was behind everyone's skill level since I was playing Warzone while they were practicing. I quickly caught up to where everyone's skill level was and competed in my first official college match in February 2020. I played as an honorary student for the University of Waterloo . I did not perform well and was insanely disappointed. I lost a lot of viewers after that after they saw I could not convert my skill in practice into high pressure games.

Since then, I have been practicing a lot with my friends and I got picked up onto RoseBlade on March 19th, 2021 and we competed in qualifiers for the CoD Rec League and qualified as a team for the Silver division. On March 31st, 2021, I joined inConsistent Gaming. They are a brand new organization and offered me a spot to coach a Challengers team. This is a big opportunity for both me and my teammates. The teammates that I grew up playing with, are now semi-professional and are only 1 strong tournament performance away from being looked at to play professionally. This is also a major opportunity for me as I get experience as a coach. I'm glad it is a team of my friends and old teammates as I do not have to teach a brand new team new strategies. They all know the plays that I like to run and I mostly just need to go over gameplay review with them. After inConsistent Gaming's first tournament, I left RoseBlade as I would no longer have time to compete with them multiple times a week and I joined a Call of Duty Rec League Gold team. This team change may seem pointless but the Gold team has stronger teammates, stronger opponents, better viewership and we only play 1 time a week. These all combine to make that a great opportunity and the time does not interfere with coaching which is my main priority.
